Boxplot¶

A boxplot is a standardized way of displaying the distribution of data based on a five number summary (“minimum”, first quartile (Q1), median, third quartile (Q3), and “maximum”). It can tell you about your outliers and what their values are. For more information on boxplot, you can refer to https://towardsdatascience.com/understanding-boxplots-5e2df7bcbd51

Decision boundary for logistic regression.¶

For logistic regression, the decision boundary appears when $$p(x) = {\exp(\beta_0 + \beta_1 x_1 + ...+ \beta_m x_m) \over 1 + \exp(\beta_0 + \beta_1 x_1+ ...+ \beta_m x_m)} = 0.5.$$ This happens when $\beta_0 + \beta_1 x_1 + ...+ \beta_m x_m = 0$.

On the bounday $$\beta_0 + \beta_{income} x_{income} + \beta_{balance} x_{balance} = 0,$$ when $x_{balance}= 0$, $income= -\beta_0/ \beta_{income}$; when $x_{balance}= 2500$, $income= -(\beta_0 + 2500\beta_{balance})/ \beta_{income}$.

Therefore, the boundary is a line that across the two points.
